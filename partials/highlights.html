Program Highlights

“It has allowed me to expand my horizons outside of the classroom and give first‐hand experiences to my students. I feel that it is important that we ‘practice what we preach’ and this experience will allow me to do just that. I gained a great appreciation for NOAA, not just in fisheries science but the NOAA Corps as well.”
- Jennifer Petro, TAS 2013

2013 was another successful year for the NOAA Teacher at Sea Program! Below are a few program highlights. Read the entire report here: 2013 NOAA Teacher at Sea Report (PDF, 1.3MB)

  • Teachers sent to sea: 35
  • Days at Sea: 520
  • Research Hours: 6200
  • States represented: 24
  • Active Alumni : 300+
  • Outreach events: 25
  • Science blogs: 200
  • Graduate Credits Awarded: 16
  • Media (articles, radio, TV): 65
